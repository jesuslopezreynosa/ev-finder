# Backstory

My dream car since I was in High School was a Tesla Model S, but when I finally began my career and could afford a nice car, I wanted something more unique...

So it all started back in April 2022 when I picked up my first new car, a 2022 Polestar 2 RWD Long Range.

![Polestar 2 Charging](/article-images/polestar-2-charging.jpeg)*-Testing out a new (at the time) ARCO / Freewire charging station in Stockton, CA-*

Around this time, have already I worked from home for a few years, so I let my partner take my car to work since he commuted around 40 miles daily.

Unfortunately in December 2022, I hydroplaned and totaled the Polestar.

In late December 2022, since I travel to see my family fairly often, I needed a car. Around this time dealerships have been marking up EVs, so I turned to Tesla. I leased a 2023 Tesla Model 3 RWD for 3 years.

Come around August 2025, I wanted to turn in the Tesla early for multiple reasons, and I got a hell-of-a-deal on a 2023 Mustang Mach-E.

![Mach-E at new GM Energy Station in Lodi, CA](/article-images/mach-e-gm-energy-lodi.jpeg)*-Visiting a recently opened GM Energy station at a Pilot Flying J in Lodi, CA-*

I have never been a car person, but driving EVs has completely changed that. I feel more in control, I love that I can zip around and I really enjoy learning about how these things work.

I ended up making this web app / micro-blog since I usually ramble to my friends and family about my EV experiences, and the most frequent question I get is, "Jesus, which EV should I consider?"

And honestly, that's a very difficult question, it depends if you live in a home or an apartment, where in California you live due to energy pricing, your commute, etc.

Unfortunately many car websites mislabel EV information, don't have complete information or lack fringe details like, "Does this car support Phone Key?" or "Does this vehicle support Plug & Charge."

All the data you see is from my scouring the internet, manufacturer specification documents, EPA data, etc. It is broken down by trim to make it easier to get detailed information about EVs.

The information is far from complete, and one day I will probably write a script that handles parsing and validation this information, but for the moment these are the vehicles that I like to compare and suggest to people.

There will be more pages that go into details about owning an EV like understanding different battery chemistries, how charging is in California or tips and tricks, but those will trickle down over time.
